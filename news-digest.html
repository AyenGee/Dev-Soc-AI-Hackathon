Good news for people who need a new driving licence card in South Africa

  • South Africa's Department of Transport faced a backlog of 756,227 driving licence cards due to a three-month breakdown of the country's only card printer between February and May 2025.
  • The printer is 27 years old and caused significant delays in licence card production.
  • The department initially planned to clear the backlog within four to six months starting 8 May 2025, targeting clearance between September and November 2025.
  • By 11 August 2025, the backlog was still large at 539,947 cards, with a daily clearance rate too slow to meet the original timeline.
  • By 13 September 2025, the backlog was reduced to 336,028 cards, with an increased daily clearance rate of about 6,179 cards due to overtime work.
  • If the faster pace continues, the backlog could be cleared by mid-November or December 2025.
  • Between 8 May and 15 September 2025, over 1.3 million cards were printed, averaging about 10,058 cards per day, aided by staff overtime.
  • Normal card production capacity is 7,440 cards per day, but full potential is around 22,320 cards daily; current output is still only about half of full capacity.
  • Even with improvements, the wait time for a new driving licence card is around two months, longer than the usual two to four weeks.
  • The Government Printing Works (GPW) will provide a backup printing solution by October 2025 to prevent future single-machine delays.
  • There is public scrutiny over the decision to use a specialized and costly printer, and calls for Parliament to investigate potential mismanagement or corruption.
  • The Department of Transport states centralizing card printing at one location was intended to reduce fraud and corruption in licence card production.
